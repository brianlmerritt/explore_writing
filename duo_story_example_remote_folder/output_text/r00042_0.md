# Duo

Anita gripped the doorframe. "Need help?"

Cleo's left arm fought the black satin, wrenching it backward. The dress sat on her like a crumpled shadow. "Why can't I be the dead one? Yes, I, I—" Her jaw locked. "No."

Anita crossed the room. "Let me be a bit of Leo. Let me help you get dressed."

"He was my right arm. He is my right arm." Cleo's voice rose against the small silence. "I can't—I can't do this without—"

"You can. You're doing it now." Anita guided the left arm free, shook the satin straight. "We used to say Leo was your right arm, and that is—was—true. Same thing."

"How? Half of me is—"

Anita lifted the empty sleeve. Turned the fabric under. Pinned it flat inside itself so it lay at Cleo's side, clean. Not hidden. Not on display. "You're still you."

"Did you and Dad really think the insufferable twins would last forever?"

"We still do. Every day."

"Then what am I now? What is Leo?"

"You are my daughter. He is my son. Your brother. Always. Always always."

"She's asked me three times why she couldn't be the dead one."

"I know."

"My heart is ripped right out but it's still beating somewhere and yet I'm still alive and she's still—"

"It's like we're back in the surgery. Waiting for their separation. Hoping. Not knowing."

"Yes."

--------------------------

Daffodils, tulips, roses, carnations laid before the foot of the grave. Cleo stood apart in the black satin, clutching daisies against her chest with her one arm. Anita pressed her hands together until the bones blanched, because Cleo needed her and Adrian needed her and the wanting to throw herself in was not something she could afford, and so she held.

"You are in me." Cleo's voice rose against the silence. "I am in you."

--------------------------

In the mirror: black satin suit, black stilettoes, a black lacy hat festooned with daisies set jauntily on top.

"Ladies and gentlemen, giving their keynote: Leo and Cleo. 'Life after Depth.'"

"Are we ready, Leo?"

"We are."
