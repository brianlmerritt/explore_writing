Duo

Anita appeared in the doorway. Cleo was wrestling a black satin dress, the empty sleeve a tangled knot behind her back.
“I can’t.”
“Let me help you.”
“Why can’t I be the dead one? Yes, I, I… no. I didn’t mean that.”
Anita crossed to her. “Let me be a bit of Leo and get this on you.” She freed the arm, then pinned the sleeve flat against the shoulder, where it belonged. The dress sat on Cleo like a crumpled shadow.
“He was in me. He is in me.” Cleo’s hand pressed her right shoulder. “Did Leo really steal my arm?”
“No. The surgeons separated you. He never stole a thing.” Anita smoothed the satin. “We said Leo was your right arm, and that is – was – true. Your father called you the insufferable twins.”
Cleo stared at the pinned cloth. “What am I now? What is Leo?”
Anita cupped her cheek. “You are our daughter. Leo is still our son, and your brother. Always! Always always!”

“Have you slept?”
“Not once.”
“I keep reaching for him in the dark.”
“I know.”
“My heart is ripped right out, but it’s still beating, somewhere.”
“It’s like we are back in the surgery, waiting for their separation. Hoping. Not knowing.”
“Yes.”
“We’ll find a way.”
“Will we?”

--------------------------

Daffodils, tulips, roses, carnations banked the fresh earth. Cleo stood a little apart, daisies clutched in her one arm against the black satin. Anita swallowed the urge to fall into the grave. Her daughter and husband needed her, so she stood. Then Cleo’s voice rose against the silence: “You are in me. I am in you.”

--------------------------

She met her own eyes in the glass: a black satin suit, stilettos, a lacy hat festooned with daisies set jauntily on top. She tilted the hat. Offstage: “Our next speaker, Cleo and her brother Leo, on ‘Life after Depth.’”
“Are we ready, Leo?”
“We are.”
