Anita popped her head in the doorway.  “Do you need any help?”
“Can you make Leo come back?  Why can’t I be the dead one?”
Clutching the frame, Anita peered at her daughter through a veil of tears.
Cleo always wore bright colours, often clashing.  Today her black satin dress sat awkwardly on her like a crumpled shadow. 
“Do you really wish you were dead?”
“Yes, I, I… no. I am the only Leo left.”
“We all loved Leo.  He is in our hearts too.”
“Yes, your hearts.  But he was in me.  He is in me.  We were split, but stayed together.”
“I know. We know.  Let me be a bit of Leo and help you get dressed.”
Cleo’s arm was trapped inside the dress, so Anita gently prised it out.  The clip holding the empty sleeve had fallen out, she picked it up and pinned the sleeve down, making sure it wasn’t hiding the lack of arm, but poised just right to not make a show of it.
“Leo was my other arm.”
“You do really well, better all the time. Dad and I and Leo are so proud of you.”
“Did Leo really steal my arm?”
“No, course not. When the surgeon separated you, your other arm hadn’t had a chance to grow properly.  We said Leo was your right arm, and that is – was – true.  Now dad and I can be your right arm, forever you need us.”
“Mom?  What dad called us?”
“The insufferable twins?”
“Yes, that.  What am I now? What is Leo?”
“You are Cleo, our daughter.  Always!  And Leo is our son and your brother.  Always always!”
--------------------------
“How is she taking it?”
“Badly, like the rest of us.  Worse.”
“How are you holding up?”
“I’m not. Not really.  My heart is ripped right out, but it’s still beating, somewhere. Yet I’m still alive.”
“Yes, it’s like we are back in the surgery, waiting for their separation.  Hoping.  Not knowing.”
“Yes.”
--------------------------
Bouquets of bright flowers, daffodils and tulips, roses and carnations, all lay before the foot of the grave. Anita’s eyes were only for Cleo, who held Leo’s favourite daisies in her left arm, pressed tightly against the satin dress.  The need, the urge to throw herself into the grave would have to wait. Cleo needed her.  Adrian needed her.  
Cleo’s voice rose against the silence “Bye bye Leo.  You are in me. I am in you.”
--------------------------
Cleo checked her reflection in the mirror.  Black satin suit, black stilettoes, black lacy hat festooned with daisies set jauntily on top.
The backstage speaker announced, “The next Ted Talk is by Leo and Cleo on *Life after Depth*.“
“Are we ready Leo?”
“We are.”
